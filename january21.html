January 2021

Won't Live Here - 10/10

by Daniel Caesar

This song is in my opinion one of the best singles Daniel Caesar has released. The piano and his voice are one and it is honestly beautiful. Overall I think he has one of the best voices in the pop/R&B genre. I often wind down to his music because of the use of the piano and harmonies and melodies. I 10/10 recommend him for sleep time.





Likes:

I like the fact that he put in fake audience noise in the beginning of the song because it makes it feel like you are listening to the song with other people. You feel included in the song and the experience which is very special. The piano is amazing and ties in perfectly with the intricacies of Caesar’s voice. I like how he drags out certain words which doesn’t make this song boring like other songs f rom different artists. He also has a tooth gap which provides another piece of evidence that musical artists with a front-tooth-gap are superior. My evidence to support this theory: Daniel Caesar and Mac Demarco. Continuing he even sings at a higher pitch and the song ends with only the piano and organ.

Dislikes:

I didn’t go into this review thinking that I would award a 10/10, but I have no criticisms. I think that for the R&B genre this song has the perfect balance. I would definitely recommend this song and wish you luck in life.



KOD - 6/10

by J Cole

This album came out during the end of eighth grade and I initially only knew a few songs on it. Of course I listened more and more during the summer and I had my speaker confiscated while I was playing “Kevin’s Heart” in my cabin. I don’t know what made me a J.Cole fan, but I am now and have been for a couple years. I think he is one of the best storytellers in the rap industry, but I also meme him because he wants to be in the NBA and it’s unlikely because he’s 35 and there are only 11 active players in the league over 35. But anyway this is about music so let’s get into it.





Likes:

There are a few really great songs in this album. “KOD” has heavy 808’s and a good balance of hi-hats and lyrics round it out really well. “Photograph” has a really great variation of vocal speed which keeps it interesting. “ATM” has the most contagious beat on the album and is overall okay minus the neverending repetition of the main chorus. By far, “Kevin’s Heart” is the best song on this album. I think it has the perfect mix of drums and bass with synth in the background. “1985” is the most popular song and was my biggest surprise listening to the whole album for the first time. It has a great story and amazing lyrics which lead to his next album which will release this year called “The Fall Off”.

Dislikes:

Although there are highlights to the album, I find most of the other songs to fall behind. “Motiv8” has some of the best rapping, but the beat isn’t there. Without a strong beat, J.Cole doesn’t have what he needs to put his music over the quality line that my playlists maintain. I hope that his next album is better because there are many missed opportunities on this one.



Tommy's Party - 7/10

by Peach Pit

I don’t have much to say about Peach Pit except that they are the most Indie-Rock band that I have in my playlists. I have tried to listen to some of their other songs but have found it repetitive. Still, I found it important to introduce such an iconic indie song.





Likes:

I like the slow pace of this song and the almost sad guitar. The slow nature and the reverb on the guitar really sets the tone of the song. The continuous base and chorus are really nice, and I like vocals and the story the song is telling. When I tell people I like indie music this is one of the common songs of conversation.

Dislikes:

My main complaint with this song is how long it is. If a song is 5 minutes long in 2021 it should have some sort of beat change or it better be “Runaway” level. I think the song lacks some diversity that would put it in the top tier but it is a solid song as a whole.



Circles - 8/10

by Mac Miller

It is with a heavy heart that I review this album by the great Mac Miller. He tragically died from a drug overdose in September of 2018. Yesterday, January 19th would have been his 29th birthday. This album was in its final stages of production when Mac passed which made it, in my opinion, the best posthumous album I’ve ever heard. It follows Mac’s 2018 album, “Swimming” in which he struggled with drugs but found himself swimming through the waves to calm waters. In a way “Circles” gives me a feeling that Mac is finally at peace. He no longer has to fight with rough water and can swim in circles. You should most definitely watch the video below to learn about the symbolism of the album. A really cool video like actually go watch it now.





Likes:

I really like the start of this album which is led off with “Circles”. “Blue World” is a very upbeat song with a really good backing beat. It is the second most popular song on the album for a reason. “Good News” was the song that I listened to for the second most times in 2020. “I Can See” makes you feel like you're floating with its melodic music. “Woods” has a great beat as well. I feel like I keep saying the same thing, but besides one song, every single track has amazing vocals and unique beats. It surely would be different if Mac was alive, but it gives off the feeling that he knew what he wanted the album to consist of and it turned out to be amazing.

Dislikes:

To start, “That’s On Me” isn’t bad, but he repeats the same vocal for over a minute which does not float my boat. “Hands” has a beat that is a little too experimental for my liking on a rap/ neo-soul album. I do like the vocals on that song though. “Complicated” has a beat really similar to Khalid’s song “Talk” which is a little too poppy for me. Again the lyrics that Mac sings can make any beat stay in the green in my book. I think that when you view the album in the series of Mac Miller released projects, it shows his growth from his initial albums and despite his tragic death, it still manages to feel like he was alive to shape it exactly how he wanted it to be.



Good Days - 6/10

by SZA

I know SZA’s music from the song “Love Galore” which features Travis Scott. This song was the first taste I had of her amazing vocals and versatility. I would probably rate that song higher than “Good Days” mostly because I’ve had “Love Galore” in my playlist for a few years at this point. While I may not be a SZA stan, I do appreciate her craft and was surprised when I listened to the full song of “Good Days” after hearing the song on Tik Tok (What a shocker).





Likes:

I love the intro to this song. I feel like I’m flying when I listen to the background music in this song. SZA has an amazing voice and her lyrics are very nice. The song released as a single is a great taste of what may lie in the future of an album or project. I really just love the music in the background and SZA’s vocals root the song down from being overly repetitive.

Dislikes:

My only complaints for this song are that the 4 minutes and 39 seconds feels a little too long with no changes to the background beat of the song. I also can’t say that the lyrics were too exciting for me. I listened more to the way that the lyrics and music intertwined together. Overall the song is solid, but it isn’t even close to being a 10.



Goosebumps HVME Remix - 6/10

by HVME

Goosebumps without the remix is already a 10/10 song in my book. Out of all of Travis’s music I think it falls within his top 5 songs of all time. The song in general is top tier and the Kendrick Lamar verse puts it over the top. That being said let's get into the review. The video is 8D audio which brings me back to freshman year. Wear headphones and prepare to experience the greatness of the original release of "Goosebumps" including the Kendrick feature.





Likes:

I like how this brought a new hype to a classic rap song. Simply put, this is the party version of regular “Goosebumps”. The drop is insane and I am confident that this will be the song I hear in college. I’ve listened to this song before working out and it gets me hyped up for sure.

Dislikes:

I don’t have many dislikes, but my main criticism is that they removed the Kendrick verse. It added so much to the original and taking it out makes the song feel slightly repetitive. My other criticism is that it isn’t too crazy which has its pros and cons. On one hand, the song keeps its roots and still sounds pretty close to the original. On the other hand, the simple change in the beat gets boring after the first few listens. The beat still hits hard, but it isn’t the same after a week or two of listening to it. I gave it a 6 because I really appreciate the new twist on a song that I already love, but I think it isn’t nearly as good as the raw original and it removed such a key part of the song. Hopefully it is better when it is mixed with other songs.



Scorpion - 7/10

by Drake

I have a story for you once again. Summer of 2018 my family took a trip to Italy and “Scorpion” dropped the day/night I was arriving. Italy time was 6 hours ahead of the East Coast, so when I woke up at 6 in the morning in Venice I immediately checked Spotify for Drake’s new release. I didn’t find it at the time and went out to do tourist things for the morning. Then we came back at lunch for a little jet-lag nap and I listened to “Scorpion” in one of the most comfortable beds and nicest hotel rooms I’ve ever been in. Overall probably one of my favorite release listening experiences.





Likes:

The songs that I like on this album, I really like. “Emotionless” is a classic for me. The piano intro into the 808 and kick beat is perfect. The sampling in the back is perfect in contrast to Drake’s articulate lyrics. “God’s Plan” blew up on a scale probably equal to “Sicko Mode” by Travis Scott. Initially I thought the iconic lyrics that come perfectly at the beat drop are, “I only love my bed and momma, I’m sorry” , but really Drake sings. “I only love Mahbed and my momma, I’m sorry”. Drake reveals that he has a son on this album which was monumental news. “I’m Upset” has a beat that puts a scowl on my face: it’s so good it’s dirty. “8 Out of 10” has an almost old fashioned beat. “Mob Ties” is also on my playlist, but it’s not too high up on my list of songs from this album. “Can’t Take a Joke” also features a fast 808 + Hi Hat beat and neutral rapping from Drake. I think it has a great balance. “Sandra’s Rose” is my favorite song on Disc 1 of the album which is split in two parts. I love the beat of this song the most. “Is There More” is another honorable mention. I like the unnatural beat and the lyrics are consistent. “Peak”, “Blue Tint”, and “In My Feelings” were the two honorable mentions. Besides the songs that I mention below, everys other song on Disk 2 is just next level for me. I still have these songs more than Disk 1 songs on my playlist. Overall I liked the second half much more, but it still provided much needed Drake hits for the summer of ‘18 and the years since.

Dislikes:

There are a few songs that I disliked: “Survival” - too lyrical, “Elevate” - don’t like the chorus that Drake chose, “Talk Up” - too much going on in the background that make Drake’s lyrics seem overly aggressive, “Don’t Matter To Me” isn’t a bad song, but I can’t listen to anything with Michael Jackson in it without thinking about the terrible things he did with children, “Final Fantasy” - don’t like the beat. Lastly, “March 14” is a wonderfully written song that reveals Drake’s son to the world and the struggles Drake faces with Adonis's mother and his internal battles. I do like the piano that follows the lyrical first half of the song.



Dead Man Walking - 2/10

by Brent Faiyaz

I initially heard Brent Faiyaz on Tik Tok (Big surprise). Tik Tok truly is responsible for so many social and cultural changes in my generation. Music is going to be extremely different now and I hope there will be a ton of more independent artists. His first song that I heard was “Darling I don’t Wish you Well” which is still unreleased, but is on Soundcloud which you can listen to below. Overall my verdict on Brent as an artist is still up in the air.


doja burger · Brent Faiyaz - Darling / Wish you well



Likes:

I like the way this song starts. It drags the intro out with high pitched strings that wind up and down. I think the lyrics and beat interact pretty well with each other. I don’t have too many positive things to say about it.

Dislikes:

For starters the intro strings are played throughout the song, which I am not a fan of. I feel like it makes the song feel like one long continuous chorus which is coupled with Brent’s repeating of the same line. I just get bored fast with this song, and while the beat is okay, it isn’t enough to save the song. I think the song had a lot of potential by keeping the intro going during the first verse, dropping, and then continuing the same intro in the back of the song. Some of the drums used just don’t sound good at all. I intended to give this a decent rating, but as I’ve been listening to it I realized it’s just not that good.



This Charming Man - 8/10

by The Smiths

I don’t know how I listened to this song, but I know it’s a classic. I don’t think it needs much intro and with that I present my review on “This Charming Man”.





Likes:

I’m a big fan of this song. The intro is immediately recognizable and contagious. My legs just start bouncing. The lyrics are airy which is typical for the Smiths, but they fit perfectly with the upbeat music. I like the timing of the delivery of the lyrics too. The chorus varies pace and words are dragged out which is accompanied by lead guitar. I don’t get bored of this song even after listening to it over and over again while writing this. It’s only 2 minutes and 42 seconds which I think is perfect. Current songs are longer and I think I get bored of them easierFor anyone who doesn’t know this song, drop everything and go listen.

Dislikes:

I don’t have any criticisms for this song, but I wouldn’t rate it a 10. Give it a listen for yourself to decide and let me know. Contact info in the footer homies.



True That - check in with me in a few weeks/10

by Michael Cera

When I heard that the Michael Cera was a released musician I almost ran around my room. I had first seen him grow up on “Arrested Development” which I absolutely recommend. It can be found on Netflix, but this is a music review website not a TV show review website. I knew that he played music from a viral meme of him being awkward in public which is in part why I think people like him so much. I feel like he’s awkward without embarrassment which is very difficult for many people. Below is the meme that I am referencing. I was shocked by what I heard when I listened to his 2014 album for the first time.





Likes:

Never in a million years would I expect Michael Cera to create such a musical masterpiece. There is something about how the album hosts such a variety of sounds and instruments that makes it enjoyable. There are sad piano songs, like “Moving In” which has a bellowing piano that is muffled but belligerent with heavy keystrokes. Sidebar that was a crazy description bellowing and belligerent wow SAT words. The few songs with vocals are folky and the harmonies in combination with the soft guitar make me feel comfortable like I’m a dog in the sun. I could only identify Cera’s voice in “Clay Pigeons”, but it’s not terrible. His musical talent is apparent and I like the vocals he chose in his songs. “Steady Now” is one of his indie-esk songs which is honestly not bad. Would I add it to my playlist, no, but I could see others liking it.

Dislikes:

“What Gives(...I Can’t Live Like This)” is just a series of uncoordinated crashing and squeaky uncoordinated instruments. Audibly It’s extremely unpleasurable, but it conveys a story. Cera is tired of living in chaos and noise that doesn’t work together. Lyrical Genius. I’ve listened to the album a few times and I’m left feeling comforted and a little confused. I don’t really know what to make of this album: it has no lyrics and lyrics, piano and guitar, folk and classical. I feel like it suits Cera’s awkward personality and it is pleasurable to listen to. I just need more time to see if I really do enjoy it which is why I rated it a check in with me in a few weeks/10.



Dark Red- 10/10

by Steve Lacy

As previously mentioned in my review of Steve Lacy’s album “The LoFi-s”, Steve Lacy mastered, produced, and recorded this song on GarageBand which blows my mind. He actually sings at the different pitches you can hear through tons of vocal recordings. I like Steve and his alt style, but sometimes his music is too experimental for me. In this case, it is just right on my scale.





Likes:

This song is really special. When I first listened to it many years ago I remember being frustrated that I couldn’t find anything else that sounds like it which you would expect with music. But I still find myself getting upset that I can’t find more music that matches the general sound of a song I like. Anyway, I like the lyrics in this song, in part because they are easy to remember, but also because the way Lacy sings them is perfectly drawn out and it makes me feel it in my chest when he sings, “Something baaaaad about to happen to me” perfectly on time with the beat changing from the drum intro to the full song. I like the drums and the chorus that he sings. Before each verse he stops the drums and guitar for just a second and then starts the verse. It really is very impressive that he managed to make this on GarageBand because of the drums and guitar. Mad respect.

Dislikes:

I don’t have a single dislike for this song. The beginning, middle, and end are all perfect and that makes it a 10 for me. I could listen to this song time after time



Sunflower- 8/10

by Vampire Weekend

I listened to this song for the first time when I was driving with my dad going to pick up pizza in town and I was shocked. Vampire Weekend has a cult following and I was not about to get wrapped up in it. However this one song will forever mean something to me and I like their song “A-Punk” which I had listened to for many years snowboarding without realizing who the band even was.





Likes:

The intro guitar of this song is insane. There are two guitarists who almost speak to each other with their rifts. I like how the guitar rift goes from higher to lower and the vocals remain balanced in the middle. The non-words from the band and the lyrics from the lead singer, Ezra Koenig, mesh in a beautiful symphony of sound. I really like this song and it has withstood the test of one year of listening to it which isn’t too bad. I think the song never gets boring and the constant change in drums and guitar and everything else going on makes it a really enjoyable listen.

Dislikes:

The only development I have encountered is that sometimes it doesn’t quite match my mood. When I want to listen to indie it usually means a more melodic laid back vibe rather than the exciting rifts that are in the song. It still is a great song, but I have yet to make a playlist that it fits in, in the right way. Right now it’s my little ugly duckling that will be beautiful someday. I didn’t give it a higher rating because I’m just not too enthusiastic about it.



Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight- 7/10

by Travis Scott

In my opinion this album gets way too much hate and there are countless great songs. Compared to other Travis projects, I think this was a little more rushed than other projects which were released at a much higher quality. Contrary to belief, I will go on record saying that I like “Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight” more than “Days Before Rodeo”. I think it is important to ignore public opinion and make your own opinion about the album.





Likes:

There are countless BANGERS on this playlist: “Goosebumps”, “sdp Interlude”, “Pick up the Phone”, “Way Back”, and “Pick up the Phone”. Those 5 songs alone make the album significant for my music listening history. “Goosebumps” is one of the top 3 most known Travis Scott songs of all time. “The Ends” and “Way Back” are great songs to start the album with and they set a good tone for the rest of what you would expect the album to sound like. “Through the late Night” has a next level Kid Cudi feature that is hidden from the tracklist. Travis often doesn’t put features in the song title to make his listeners appreciate his music for it’s quality rather than it’s features which I respect to a high degree.

Dislikes:

With all the good, there is also a lot of bad like “First Take”, which is not my style at all. Travis’s vocals are flat. The middle of “lose” is a weird section of the song which takes away from the potential of the song. “Guidance” isn’t a Travis song in my mind because it has an upbeat rhythm that doesn’t fit with the album. “Coordinate” isn’t bad, but I think it doesn't fit into my definition of what Travis’s music sounds like because it has such a weak beat when he has other songs with outrageous beats which are shown in literally every “Astroworld” song. Overall, I feel like the album could have some more unity which is the thing this project lacks when compared to Travis’s other albums.



Man on the Moon: The End of Day (I)- 8/10

by Kid Cudi

When I listened to “Man on the Moon III”, I listened to the first and second before to set the tone for the third. This album really is something special. There are songs on this album that literally made Kid Cudi’s career. This was Cudi’s first album and before I listened to it, in its entirety of 15 songs on the regular album, I had already listened to a variety of the songs on “Man on the Moon: The End of Day”. Cudi is known for his lyrical genius along with his perfect balance of melodic beats. The album is about dreams which is clear in the title of the first song “In My Dreams(Cudder Anthem)”. This being said, when a song has (nightmare) after it, it is about the nightmares in Cudi’s life and are much darker which is tied to Mr. Rager who is the darker side of Cudi in Man on the Moon 2. Even 12 years after this album was released, it still is just as good as the day it was released.





Likes:

This album is in my opinion the best of the trilogy of the “Man on the Moon” releases. “In My Dreams(Cudder Anthem)” is deeply melodic and the end of the song features a PSA type announcement from Cudi himself. “Simple As…” is one of my favorite songs on the album. I love how he makes a beat out of the numbers 1,2, and 3 and the letters a,b,c. “Solo Dolo(Nightmare)” is eerie but the baseline roots the song down and it creates the perfect tension. “Day ‘N’ Night(nightmare)” is a notorious college party remix song and I listened to it many times in my underclassmen years of high school. “Alive(Nightmare)” and “Cudi Zone” are both top notch songs. “Make Her Say” features “Poker Face” by Lady Gaga while also hosting a Kanye West feature. “Pursuit of Happiness(Nightmare)” has a pretty sick guitar solo. “Up Up & Away” is a great way to end the album because it is very upbeat and completes a cycle from dark to light which is a constant battle in the album and in Cudi’s life.

Dislikes:

“Heart of a Lion(Kid Cudi Theme)” isn’t my kind of song. It is still a good song, but I think he has other, better songs. “My World” is a little monotone even though I like the beat. I don’t think the album is perfect by any means, but I think it’s damn close. I like most of Cudi’s beats and when he strays too far I think it doesn’t fit with the flow of the album, but over time he has reeled that in by releasing more experimental music over time in subsequent albums. Kid Cudi is a rap god and I think many people recognize his genius and I hope you do too.

I still think that you should never doubt Kanye, because he always turns it around and says “haha look at you, you never should have doubted me. I’m Kanye!” This clearly isn’t Kanye quality and I hope the next music he releases is much better. The video below is the music video of the remix of "Nah Nah Nah" which is better, but not by much. I reviewed the original song just so you know.





Likes:

The only thing I like in the entire song is the fact that Kanye samples Star Wars Revenge of the Sith when he sings, “Don’t jump Anakin I have the higher ground” followed by Obi-Wan Kanobi saying it in the movie. I don’t know why he sampled it and I didn’t think that he could ruin a cinematic masterpiece in such a prosperous movie franchise, but he did it.

Dislikes:

I cannot find a single thing to say that could save this song. The beat is uncoordinated with a flute. I don’t think I even need to justify why I gave it a 0, if you can’t understand why, you better start listening to my recommendations because it is absolutely terrible.
