October 2020

This is a cool slowed + reverb LoFi version of "Chandelier" and I like it even better than the original


Likes:

In general I really enjoyed the results of Paquin's first song. I feel that sometimes when an artist plays a rift it can sometimes produce a sub-par song, yet "Chandelier" was a solid track. The lyrics established a good baseline for the future of his music which can be seen throughout his recent videos of conceptual songs. I find myself not getting enough of his music and constantly looking for more rifts or tunes to listen to.

Dislikes:

The only dislike I have about "Chandelier" is the use of what I would describe as the sound a homing missile would make as it approaches its target. I wish he hadn't included it in the song simply because the rest of it is something I could fall asleep to and the one synth song he put in clashes with the rest of the song. Luckily it only comes up a few times in the song which is why I rated the song a 7. Without this sound I could have given it a 10.





Likes:

I am a huge fan of the matchup of talent in this podcast. To put a veteran artist like Mac in a call with Dayglow, an artist on the edge of establishing himself in the industry, it created the perfect environment for the podcast. It was the perfect listen for a late weeknight and served its purpose as background music as I did some homework. As a huge fan of Mac, any new content was good content and it was interesting to hear about the effects of the pandemic on the indie music industry.

Dislikes:

I found the 45 minute podcast to drag at times when the conversation slowed naturally. Mac has a very calm demeanor which translated in the podcast. I think that the situation in general is uncomfortable when you meet someone virtually, so that likely contributed to the awkwardness. Overall, the slow pace was the only thing that I disliked, but it weighed heavily on my ranking.




Likes:

The only thing I can say that I do like is the features that the album includes. Drake consistently puts out catchy and enjoyable songs, but not on this song. Morgan Freeman is arguably one of the most shocking and beneficial additions to the album. His two songs, which are more like voice recordings, went viral on Tik Tok which helped to boost views.

Dislikes:

I'll keep this short by saying I disliked everything else on the album. I found the beats to be uncoordinated and clashing with other instruments used in songs. I was hoping for big results with Drake as a feature but "Mr. Right Now" has a weird high pitched beat mixed with 808's and hi-hats. In my opinion without the main beat, it would have been a hit. Overall disappointed and skipped most of the songs in the album and I don't plan on ever listening to one of its songs even after trying to re-listen to find a new appreciation to no avail. A big sequel album let down in the wake of high expectations.
