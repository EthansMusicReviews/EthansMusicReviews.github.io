April 2021


Likes:

The best part of this song in the intro. The smooth cut baseline mixes so well with the drums and classic turntable sound. The vocals are art with the call and response of “Can I kick it? Yes ya can.” The baseline is stripped during the verse and all that’s left is the vocals and the drums. The turntable sound captures the music of A Tribe Called Quest’s prime and I enjoy the nostalgia. Also, the intro is repeated as different vocalists start their verses which I really like. The cutting of the beat in the second vocalists verse around 2:40 is just so nice and it keeps the song fresh even three minutes in.

Dislikes:

There is one part of the song around 1:30 that does not float my boat. I think the song and what sounds like synthesised piano pitches up a little too much for my liking. A drum solo or something that fits with the beat may work better, or maybe not because the higher pitch serves as a break for the song which keeps it original. Regardless this is a classic and should be respected as such.





Likes:

I found that I liked the beat of a lot of the songs that were released, but the vocals and other instruments let some of the songs down. The beat of “Chain On” was so funky and had so much potential with strong vocals. “Bankroll” absolutely goes. The features of A$AP Rocky and A$AP Ferg bring this song to another level with Brockhampton member Merlin opening the song with strong vocals and his deep voice. The beat has a halloween feel, but the other instruments and the catchy ukulele cover the beat and make this one of the best songs on the album. “The Light” also has a great beat that is intro’d by a sick guitar riff. “What’s the Occasion?” I am a fan of, but don’t quite know what playlist to add it to. “When I Ball” samples string instruments which adds another dimension to the song, but it just doesn’t belong in one of my playlists. “Don’t Shoot Up the Party” has been bumping on my Spotify since the drop of the album on April 9th. The beat leads in so well to the drop at 40 seconds with some ringing sound that is signature to Brockhampton. The continuous beat works well in this song as a result of the various vocals and instruments added on.

Dislikes:

I really want to give this album a higher ranking, but while there may be versatility in the kind of sound in each song, there isn't in the playlists that the songs belong in. I find it hard to add multiple songs to one of my playlists and it really sucks because I really do like some of the songs in this album but there isn’t a group of songs that sound the same. It makes me think about doing a playlist restructuring in the near future.

vonnybando · TRUST-sleepy hallow

Likes:

The instrumental for this song fits perfectly with the tone of the song. The lyrics are about heartbreak and I think the harp and drums give it a perfect place in the rap genre. I know all the words to the chorus now, and I really like the lo-fi beat with the mix of drums and 808’s. The interlude, chorus, verse, chorus, verse, chorus structure gives the song the perfect length. Although it’s unreleased, the verses are long and high quality and I wish this song would be released on streaming services, but I doubt it.

Dislikes:

I really have no criticism for this song and constantly put it on when I’m with friends or driving alone. I definitely recommend this song.





Likes:

The intro to this song is absolutely magical. The slow guitar pitched down, the music and vocal alterations are so perfect. There is a sound that sounds almost like a record skipping and the drum beat over it gives the song a heartbeat. Travis’s vocals are catchy and clear to understand. I think his production of the drums and how they speed up in the same musical timing is phenomenal.

Dislikes:

I really have no complaints about this song. There aren’t any areas where I feel like Travis over, or under-did vocals, drums, or synth. Maybe this song holds personal value, but I find this to be an easy 10/10.





Likes:

Specifically I like the chorus of the song. I think the hook is genius and the lyrics are so hard. “I’m living the sweet life, London Tipton” is an allusion to the show “The Sweet Life of Zach and Cody” in which, London Tipton’s father owned the hotel that the show takes place in. When I heard that bar I knew I had to review it.

Dislikes:

Unfortunately, I feel like this song falls in a few places. I know that I can’t hold a leak to the same standards as a full release because Juice didn’t have opportunity to finish the song out. That is why I feel like it is incomplete. The intro and ending are almost 20 seconds each and the chorus takes a long time so the verses feel incomplete. The drums are good, but there still could have been another element added to the song. I feel like a feature would round the song out well, but we will never know.
