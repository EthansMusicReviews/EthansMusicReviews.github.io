February 2021

Dunno - Recorded at Spotify Studios NYC - 8/10

by Mac Miller

In my opinion, Mac Miller is the greatest loss to the modern music industry out of all of the deaths in the past few years. He had so much musical potential and he was a happy person. Old videos show how candid he was and I think that shows through his music which often showcased his musical abilities. This song is a gem that I found just looking through Spotify and I’m eternally grateful.





Likes:

I definitely have a soft spot for piano and physical instruments over electronic instruments which is why “Dunno” has more impact to me. Mac has a pretty good voice for a rapper and while it can be repetitive because his vocal range is limited, the piano serves him well in this song.

Dislikes:

I don’t have any dislikes for this song but I don’t think it’s a 10 simply because it doesn’t fit in my playlist. I don’t listen to many stripped songs with only piano so it doesn’t fit right in any of my current playlists, but maybe one day it will.



Now You Know - 7/10

by Will Paquin

Well, I thought it would be a few weeks, but the next time that Will Paquin released music was almost half a year after his first release. I can imagine it must have been hard seeing as though he is still a student in college and had to record his vocals in his car which he revealed in a TikTok live the night of his new song’s release. It was a super cool experience to listen to him play random riffs and talk to fans, but I also felt like he was putting off imposter syndrome vibes. I also then watched the live release on youtube and was taken aback by the decent use of synths and you can watch the TikTok live below.





Likes:

I like the fingerpicking pattern of this song although it sounds a lot like “Chandelier”. I think that the natural progression of the song is really good and Paquin leads with the guitar instead of drums which makes his music so special. I actually liked the synth that he used in this song and his section of anger at 50 seconds into the song is passionate and fits the meaning behind the song. I like the pauses that he puts in the song and the synth really adds to the song. I think that overall the words behind the song have more meaning. Overall I liked this song much more than “Chandelier” and I look forward to whatever songs he released in the (hopefully) near future.

Dislikes:

My list of dislikes for this song is pretty short. Firstly, I think he layered his voice for the first line or two of vocals which gives it a weird sound that to me sounds disconnected and hollow. This was fixed only a few lines into the song, but I could do without it. I know that it was his intention to keep the same core part of the song very similar to “Chandelier” but after months I feel like it is a new take on an old song. I have heard his other song ideas and they are more different to each other than “Now You Know” is to “Chandelier”. Otherwise I liked this song a lot more than his other and I think he’s on a good path for more music!



Been That - 8/10

by Jay Critch

Two weekends ago my friend Ronan introduced me to Jay Critch and by all means this song goes harder than any of the songs that he showed me.





Likes:

The intro to this song is unique and also is mixed perfectly with the 808’s that lead in the song. With your bass turned up the harp intro combined with Critch’s powerful vocals and the song is insane. Usually the same backing beat isn’t my favorite but because the song is only two minutes I think it works perfectly.

Dislikes:

My main complaint is the symbol synth sound that is used throughout the song that starts around 36 seconds. It isn’t horrible but sounds like a record skipping and I feel like he could have put another sample in there. The strength of the 808’s covers up the synth well but it definitely is a detractor from the song.

Pluto Projector - 8/10

by Rex Orange County

Rex Orange County has been on my radar for artists for a good while since sleep away camp maybe 3 years ago when a girl told me she liked him. When I got home I listened to his songs like “Bestfriend” and “Loving Is Easy” but it wasn’t quite my taste until a year or so ago. One of the things that I admire the most about Rex is that in the beginning of the pandemic he would have Instagram Live concerts essentially for an hour or more at a time seamlessly switching from piano to guitar. He was just a person who wanted to spread some light in the chaos and I appreciated it a lot.





Likes:

While this is only one song out of the album, I chose to focus on it because I think it can be overlooked. The guitar in the beginning serves as the perfect introduction to the song with Rex’s insecurities about his relationship. The next verse talks about how he has “boyish thoughts” and admits to his faults in an amazing climax of a brief 20 second buildup of a chorus of echoey singing. Rex talks about wanting an encore of his life imagining marrying his girlfriend who he has since broken up with. The last minute of the song is a beautiful strings instrumental section that honestly takes my breath away. It ebbs and flows up and down and I can feel it in my chest.

Dislikes:

I don’t like the lulls in this song. There are parts of the song that can drag a bit which I may feel because of how much I like the instrumental aspects of the song. My only other complaint is that at the end of the song, the strings end and there is a pitched down recording of Rex that interrupt the flow that was created right before it. I would have rather it simply end with the strings, and I almost never listen to the end anyway because I skip past it.



Ronan Waters X - cool/10

by Instagram Live

In between period 7 and period 8 on Tuesday February 16th, 2021 as I was scrolling through my social media I saw a notification that made me literally jump out of my chair: Ronan.waters is now going live with Jay.Critch. This past weekend Ronan put me on to Jay Critch’s music and I haven't stopped listening. Imagine my surprise when he was literally talking to the rapper who he had just shown me. It was insane. WARNING: profanity used in the video below.





Likes:

I was impressed that Ronan was able to come off so smooth given how shocked he was that Jay Critch actually accepted his request to go live. For around one minute they talked about school and it was just super cool to see my friend talking to a famous artist. There is no way that I would have been able to come off as smooth and Ronan even told me afterwards that he was shaking when he joined the IG live. Immediately after he left the live I face timed him and it was clear that he was still in shock. Overall, it was the highlight of this Tuesday and likely this week.

Dislikes:

It was just too short. I think Ronan and I both would have liked for the interaction to be a little longer, but the limited time that was shared was great.



Rodeo - 7/10

by Travis Scott

This album in general I find to be Travis’ worst album released, not because it’s bad, but because the others are so good. I would rather say less about this album except the general foreshadowing that while “Rodeo” is good, “Days Before Rodeo” is 10 times better so be on the lookout for a review coming soon.





Likes:

There are some absolute bangers like “Oh My Dis Side(feat. Quavo)”, “3500(feat.Future & 2 Chainz)”, “Impossible”, and “Maria I’m Drunk (feat. Justin Beiber & Young Thug)”. While these songs are all in my playlists, nothing can top the ART that is “90210 (feat. Kacy Hill)”. This song is widely regarded as the best Travis song of all time, and for good reason. There is a massive beat switch halfway through the song, although I like the second first half better. The vocals from Hill and the production of the song are phenomenal together. The bars are crazy and I know without a doubt that it is the best part of the entire album. “Rodeo” went Platinum in the US in 2017.

Dislikes:

I think that this album is a great demonstration of the capacity that Travis once had. He had amazing songs, but he also had songs that were not even close to his level. “Ok Alright” is lacking main drums or a good guitar chorus. The song is just lacking which is a common trend in other songs like “Never Catch Me”. Other songs have so much potential but I think Travis dropped the ball. “Never Catch Me” has a mean guitar in the intro and then the main song just completely falls off. It is an important stepping stone when looking at the path that Travis took to achieve the fame he has today, but it is clearly the worst when it comes to his entire discography.



Kids See Ghosts - 9/10

by KIDS SEE GHOSTS

The Duo you never knew you needed released a 7 song collaborative album in 2018. Kid Cudi hums throughout the album and Kanye brings his amazing production skills. They performed together in the video linked below in one of the best performances I’ve seen. Major FOMO. I really like the two artists together and hope they collaborate in the future again, but I think it’s unlikely.





Likes:

I like the diversity of the songs on this album, but throughout the beats are phenomenal. Kanye and Cudi both have great production skills. “4th Dimension” has so many beat changes and “Kids See Ghosts” has such a spooky xylophone beat with Cudi’s light vocals. “Fire” has one of my favorite guitar solos at the end of the song. “Cudi Montage” uses guitar as the intro which is later transformed into an angelic chorus which shows how versatile each song is.

Dislikes:

There are sections of some songs that I don’t like, but not entire songs. “Feel The Love” has a section of gunshots and random lyrics around 1:05 which I am not a fan of. Otherwise, I think the album is pretty underrated by a lot of Rap fans and hope that you enjoy it!



Rainbow Bap - 6/10

by Jaden

Jaden is pretty hit or miss. “PCH” was a great song and in the follow up album there weren’t too many hits, until his third album produced “Rainbow Bop”. I tried to listen to the other songs on all of his other albums, but some of them are too poppy for my liking. This song reminds me a lot of “PCH” and I’ll explain why further in my review.





Likes:

I really like the instruments and beat changes in this song. The guitar is nice, but the variety of kicks and drums with the addition of flute make the beat interesting throughout the 4:40 long song. The bars that he spits are also not bad and quite memorable. I stumbled upon this through spotify’s recommendations and I really liked it. Overall it’s light and enjoyable to listen to.

Dislikes:

My concern for this song is that it is going to be burnt out really fast. “PCH” was a great song, but after a month or two it got old and with that it was removed from my playlist. I think the lack of strong drums and light instrumentals like the flute will have the same effect on this song, but only time will tell.



In the Aeroplane Over the Sea - 9/10

by Neutral Milk Hotel

This song once again came to my attention through TikTok and it didn’t disappoint. Three men were standing in a bathtub singing an a capella. Upon further research, I found out that the main vocalist in the band wrote this album about his dreams and this song is about his dreams concerning Anne Frank. He read “The Diary of Anne Frank” and was overcome by depression at the end of the book because of the intimacy he felt by getting a view into her mind and life. I think the hollow feeling is shown in this song, but it comes across in a nice way.





Likes:

I really like the guitar in this song. It isn’t complicated, even I could play it, yet at the same time it is just enough to accompany the vocals. There is also a wonderful chorus that begins around 40 seconds that sounds almost like air flying past you in a plane. There are also brass instruments used in the middle of the song around 1:22. Overall the song is unique and a surprise in my opinion seeing as though I had never heard of the band before.

Dislikes:

My only dislikes are that it doesn’t quite align with any of my playlists. It has a granola kind of vibe and I don’t have a playlist to fit that YET, but I may have to make one. I also need to read into the lyrics more to fully understand the symbolism used which I will in the future. I enjoyed this surprise TikTok find for sure.



Damn - 10/10

by Kendrick Lamar

I have to be honest, I may lose some credit and respect when I reveal this secret: I had not listened to a single Kendrick Lamar album from start to finish before I reviewed this album. Luckily, I did and I can’t say that I’m surprised by how blown away I am. Kendrick is widely regarded as one of the best rappers of all time. His flows are insane and I think the way he delivers his lyrics is so unique that it is obvious when you hear his voice.





Likes:

This album had a few songs that I already heard, but there were more that I ended up liking a lot. The chorus effect on “BLOOD.” is insane and I like the way it ends with one gunshot. Instead of bringing up the songs that are classics like “DNA.”, “HUMBLE.”, “PRIDE.” and “LOYALTY.” I want to focus on the songs that surprised me. I need to mention “LOVE.” which has been in my top 5 songs of the year since I first heard it in 2017 when the album was released. Even though I clearly love “LOVE.” the song “FEEL.” blew my mind. The melodic beat and the rapping style with barely any drums is my ideal song. I don’t remember the last time that I heard a song that felt as good as this one does. “FEAR.” shows off Kendrick’s great lyricism and rapping capabilities. “GOD.” has a really good beat which I think shows a different range for him, but is still Kendrick Lamar quality.

Dislikes:

The one song I didn’t like on this album is “XXX.” I will be honest the chorus goes hard with the sirens and the aggressive rap style just works for Kendrick. I am hesitant to do this, especially because I haven’t listened to any of his other, higher ranked, albums and that could break my rating scale, but I can’t see myself giving this album anything else but a 10/10.



Assassin's Creed 4: Black Flag(Sea Shanty Edition) - Drunken Sailor/10

by various artists

Sea shanties; the music of the greatest swashbucklers in the world. From my first time watching “Pirates of the Carribean” to the game on my dad’s ipad, I have always been fascinated with pirates. Then in 2013 Abstergo Studios blessed the world with Assassin's Creed 4: Black Flag. You sail as Edward Kenway and switch from hand to hand combat to fighting with you own ship, the Jackdaw. I have now played the game 3 times and after listening to this playlist I can say that I have been inspired to 100% the game. It is my favorite single videogame of all time and I cannot stress how much I encourage you to give it a play.





Likes:

Sea shanties have a great power of being able to make you feel like you yourself are sailing on the seas. Even though this playlist is missing the sounds of waves slapping the side of your boat, sails being hoisted and lowered, and Edwards sailing commands, the playlist still is amazing. “Leave Her Johnny” is my favorite song of the entire game. “Randy Dandy Oh” and “Fish in the Sea” are the most iconic songs in the game and I could listen to the crew of my ship sing these songs for hours, and I have!

Dislikes:

I think it’s difficult to identify my dislikes in the playlist of songs because Sea Shanties are in a ranking system all their own. I can’t throw a Sea Shanty into most of my playlists, but I can make a playlist of the best Sea Shanties. One day I hope to drive my friends on a road trip and sing to these sea shanties. I would say the only song I don’t like is “Trooper and the Maid” because the main vocals are too high of a pitch for me to sing which takes away from the song because I feel that you should be the main vocalist if you sing alone. Overall I like how Sea Shanties can put me into the time of pirates and would definitely recommend Assassins Creed Black Flag, and Sea Shanties.



Gravity - 3/10

by Brent Faiyaz ft. Steve Lacy, Tyler the Creator, and DJ Dahi

When I saw the people on this song, Tyler the Creator, Brent Faiyaz, and Steve Lacy I got very excited. Tyler and Steve are both decorated artists who I have nothing but respect for, and Brent is still undecided in my book. I didn’t really know what to expect, and “Gravity” lived up to that uncertainty.





Likes:

The background guitar is clearly Steve Lacy designed and I kind of like it. The Brent Faiyaz vocals honestly work well in the chorus of the song, but it can be a little off in some lines. The Tyler vocals work really well with the beat way more than the Brent vocals.

Dislikes:

I like certain parts of the song, but there is no intro to the song which I dislike. The chorus does carry the song, but I feel like there isn’t much variety in backing music which takes away from the song as a whole. The lack of intro really put my vibes down. I was honestly let down by this release. I know that it was overall Brent’s project and I don’t really vibe with his music too well, but I was hoping to have more Tyler. This song isn’t short of potential, but it did certainly fall below my expectations.
