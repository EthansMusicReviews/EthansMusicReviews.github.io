Welcome

This is the reviews page! Here I will review the events of the week and discuss my methods for finding new music. I would like to consider my taste in music to be quite versatile, but at the start of the COVID-19 pandemic I immersed myself in the indie genera. Tik Tok has worked wonders to expand the reach of indie music some say it may be "basic". In my opinion that is far from the truth. The individual self made sounds of indie hooked me and I hope it hooks you as well

Chandelier - 7/10

by Will Paquin

Will Paquin began his music career on tik tok when he posted a video sitting on a porch with a sunset in the background. I remember scrolling on my for you page and feeling an unusual comfort when he played his first draft of his first song "Chandelier". His use of harmonics is a refreshing sound in the indie scene that drew me in immediately. Also, the background of his first video features a wood and cable railing that I hope to have in my home in the future. Overall Paquin demonstrates the power of Tik Tok in the music industry as on October 20th he posted a picture showing 1,000,000 listens on his FIRST song "Chandelier" in under one month. This kind of success for an artist who captioned one of his first videos "I dont sing so be nice" is unheard of. I am a huge fan and can't wait for Paquin to release more music.

Likes and Dislikes

Likes:
In general I really enjoyed the results of Paquin's first song. I feel that sometimes when an artist plays a rift it can sometimes produce a sub-par song, yet "Chandelier" was a solid track. The lyrics established a good baseline for the future of his music which can be seen throughout his recent videos of conceptual songs. I find myself not getting enough of his music and constantly looking for more rifts or tunes to listen to.

Dislikes:
The only dislike I have about "Chandelier" is the use of what I would describe as the sound a homing missle would make as it approaches it's target. I wish he hadn't included it in the song simply because the rest of it is something I could fall asleep to and the one synth song he put in clashes with the rest of the song. Luckily it only comes up a few times in the song which is why I rated the song a 7. Without this sound I could have given it a 10.

Talkhouse Podcast - 6/10

featuring Mac Demarco and Dayglow

The talkhouse podcast is a true treat for any indie lover. Instagram ads blessed me and advertised that Mac Demarco, one of my favorite artists, had a podcast with Dayglow an artist that I knew of but not about. In the podcast, the two artists discuss touring, the impact of COVID on the industry, and various other topics. Demarco becomes a big brother type of figure during the podcast by imparting tidbits of information with young Dayglow yet they also can share a laugh over their work.

Likes and Dislikes

Likes:
I am a huge fan of the matchup of talent in this podcast. To put a veteran artist like Mac in a call with Dayglow, an artist on the edge of establishing himself in the industry, it created the perfect environment for the podcast. It was the perfect listen for a late weeknight and served its purpose as background music as I did some homework. As a huge fan of Mac, any new content was good content and it was interesting to hear about the effect of the pandemic on the indie music industry.

Dislikes:
I found the 45 minute podcast to drag at time as the conversation lulled at points. Mac has a very calm demeanor which translated in the podcast. Overall, the slow pace was the only thing that I disliked, but it weighed heavily on my ranking.

Savage Mode 2 - 1/10

Metro Boomin and 21 Savage released their much anticipated follow up to the iconic album "Savage Mode", and I was sadly underwhelmed. Their first album yielded songs that are still on my playlist nearly four and a half years later like "X" and "Savage Mode". In their second album, I found the intro "Intro" featuring Morgan Freedman to be the best part of the album.

Likes and Dislikes

Likes:
The only thing I can say that I do like is the features that the album includes. Drake consistently puts out catchy and enjoyable songs, but not on this song. Morgan Freedman is arguably one of the most shocking and beneficial additions to the album. His two songs, which are more like voice recordings, went viral on Tik Tok which helped to boost views.

Dislikes:
I'll keep this short by saying I disliked everything else on the album. I found the beats to be uncoordinated and clashing with other instruments used in songs. I was hoping for big results with Drake as a feature but "Mr. Right Now" has a weird high pitched beat mixed with 808's and hi-hats. In my opinion without the main beat, it would have been a hit. Overall disappointed and skipped most of the songs in the album and I don't plan on ever listening to one of its songs even after trying to re-listen to find a new appreciation to no avail. A big sequel album let down in the wake of high expectations.
